# CleanYourMac

**A free, open-source, native macOS app that reclaims disk space, wipes privacy
traces, uninstalls apps cleanly, and audits your security posture — and shows you
everything before it touches a single file.**

CleanYourMac is an open-source alternative to CleanMyMac, rebuilt as a real native
SwiftUI app — not Electron, not a shell script. It reclaims disk space, clears
privacy traces, uninstalls apps down to the last leftover, and checks your security
posture, without ever deleting anything you didn't review. MIT-licensed and free
forever: no subscription, no account, no upsells.

> *Not affiliated with MacPaw. "Open-source alternative to CleanMyMac" is a
> nominative reference only.*

## Why CleanYourMac

- **Open source & free — no subscription.** MIT-licensed and genuinely free, with no
  account and no pushy upsells. Read the code, fork it, trust it — instead of paying
  ~$40/yr for a cleaner you can't inspect.
- **Truly native.** Real SwiftUI with a frosted-glass sidebar and a restrained,
  editorial "obsidian" aesthetic — a Mac app that feels like it belongs on the Mac.
- **Honest by design, no dark patterns.** Nothing is deleted without your review,
  totals are real, and disruptive actions are opt-in. Every privacy item carries an
  honest impact note like "Signs you out" or "Clears your command history."
- **Safety by architecture.** A fixed allowlist, a protected-path denylist, symlink
  canonicalization, and a minimum-depth guard mean it physically cannot target your
  home folder, your documents, or "/". Everything goes to the Trash, reversibly.

## Features

**1. Smart Scan** — One click runs every module concurrently, then presents the
findings for you to review. Nothing is deleted without your approval, and the totals
are honest.

**2. System Junk** — Reclaims disk space from user caches (`~/Library/Caches`),
application logs, Xcode DerivedData, and developer-tool caches (npm / Gradle /
CocoaPods). Everything it clears is user-owned, requires no sudo or root, and is
safely regenerated by the system.

**3. Uninstaller** — Removes an app *and* all its leftovers: Application Support,
caches, preferences (+ByHost), containers, group containers, saved state, logs, web
storage, WebKit data, cookies, launch agents, and application scripts. Each match
carries a confidence level — **HIGH** (exact bundle identifier) or **MEDIUM**
(matched by app name; review before removing) — and it refuses to touch Apple/system
apps.

**4. Large & Old Files** — Surfaces large and old files so you can review and clear
what you no longer need.

**5. Privacy** — Two things in one:
- **Trace clearing** across Safari, Chrome, Edge, Brave, Vivaldi, Firefox, Arc,
  Opera, Opera GX, Chromium, and auto-detected Electron apps — plus macOS subsystems
  like Recent Items, the QuickLook thumbnail cache, saved window state, the download
  "where-from" quarantine DB, shell/Terminal history, and crash & diagnostic reports.
  Every item has an honest impact note, and disruptive items (cookies, site data,
  open sessions, window state, shell history) are opt-in, not on by default.
- **Security & privacy audit** that flags real misconfigurations: Remote Login (SSH)
  enabled, Screen Sharing on, File Sharing on, Firewall off, FileVault off, Mac
  analytics sharing on, Guest user enabled, AirDrop discoverable by everyone,
  world-readable SSH private keys / AWS credentials / `~/.netrc`, and secrets
  possibly sitting in your shell history.

## Safety

Safety isn't a checkbox here — it's the architecture. Four guardrails work together
so the app *physically cannot* delete the wrong thing:

- **Declarative allowlist.** Cleanable locations are a fixed, known-safe list — never
  derived from the items being deleted. If a path isn't on the allowlist, it's
  off-limits, full stop.
- **Protected-path denylist.** Certain paths are *never* touched: `/`, `/System`,
  `/Library`, `/usr`, `/Applications`, and your whole home directory — including
  Documents, Desktop, Downloads, Movies, Music, Pictures, Mail, Messages, Keychains,
  Photos, and Application Support.
- **Symlink canonicalization.** Every path is fully resolved before any action, so
  nothing can use a symlink to "escape" an allowed root and reach a protected one.
- **Minimum-depth guard.** A path has to be deep enough to be a real, specific
  target. The app can never aim at something shallow and catastrophic like `/` or
  `/Users/me`.

On top of that: everything moves to the **Trash** (reversible, not a permanent
delete), and the app never removes passwords, autofill or saved cards, bookmarks,
personal documents, photos, or keychain data. This model is honest and has been
adversarially reviewed — because honesty is the brand.

**What it does *not* do:** no virus scanning or malware removal (there is no
antivirus in this app), and no "speed up your Mac / free your RAM" magic. The value
is real and bounded: reclaim disk space safely, clean privacy traces, uninstall
cleanly, and check your security posture.
